### Osteoarthritis (Sandhigata Vata): A Degenerative Condition
This is the most common form of arthritis, often associated with ageing and "wear and tear."
*   **The Vata Imbalance:** The primary cause is an aggravation of **Vata dosha**. With its dry, cold, and light qualities, imbalanced *Vata* settles in the joints and depletes the natural lubricating synovial fluid (*Shleshaka Kapha*). This leads to the bones rubbing against each other, causing degeneration of the cartilage and bone tissue (*Asthi Dhatu*).
*   **Key Symptoms:** The pain is often a dull ache that worsens with movement. There is significant stiffness (especially in the morning), restricted movement, and a characteristic cracking or popping sound (*crepitus*) in the joints.

### Rheumatoid Arthritis (Amavata): An Inflammatory Condition
This is an autoimmune disorder where the body's own immune system attacks the joints.
*   **The Role of Ama:** The root cause of *Amavata* begins in the digestive tract with a weak digestive fire (*Mandagni*). This leads to the improper digestion of food and the creation of a sticky, toxic substance called ***Ama***. This *Ama* enters the circulation, travels throughout the body, and settles in the joints. The body's immune system attacks this *Ama*, causing severe inflammation, swelling, and pain.
*   **Key Symptoms:** The pain is often sharp, migrating (moving from one joint to another), and accompanied by swelling, redness, and fever. There is a loss of appetite and a general feeling of heaviness or lethargy.

**Core Ayurvedic Therapies for Arthritis**
The treatment is chosen based on the specific type of arthritis diagnosed.
**Treatments for Osteoarthritis (Sandhigata Vata)**
The goal here is **lubrication, nourishment, and pacifying Vata**.
*   **Janu/Kati Vasti:** A specialized therapy where a ring of dough is placed over the affected joint (knee/lower back), and warm, medicated, analgesic oil is retained within it. This deeply lubricates and nourishes the joint.
*   **Pizhichil:** A deeply rejuvenating therapy where a continuous stream of warm medicated oil is poured all over the body. It is exceptionally effective for pacifying severe *Vata*.
*   **Abhyanga and Swedana:** A gentle massage with *Vata*-pacifying oils followed by a herbal steam bath helps to relieve stiffness and improve circulation.

**Treatments for Rheumatoid Arthritis (Amavata)**
The goal here is **detoxification, reducing inflammation, and digesting Ama**.
*   **Virechana (Medicated Purgation):** A *Panchakarma* therapy that is highly effective for expelling *Ama* and pacifying the inflammatory *Pitta dosha*.
*   **Patra Pinda Sweda (Elakizhi):** A therapeutic sweating treatment using poultices made of fresh anti-inflammatory leaves. It is excellent for relieving pain, swelling, and stiffness.
*   **Dhanyamla Dhara:** A therapy where a warm, fermented medicinal liquid is streamed over the body. It has powerful anti-inflammatory properties.

**The Critical Role of an Ayurvedic Diet (Pathya)**
### For Osteoarthritis (Sandhigata Vata)
*   **Favour:** Warm, moist, and nourishing foods. Include ghee, sesame oil, soups, and well-cooked grains.
*   **Avoid:** Cold, dry, and processed foods that aggravate *Vata*.

### For Rheumatoid Arthritis (Amavata)
*   **Favour:** Warm, light, and easy-to-digest foods. Use spices like ginger, turmeric, and garlic to boost digestion.
*   **Avoid:** Heavy, cold, fried, and dairy products (especially yogurt and cheese) that create *Ama*.

**Supportive Lifestyle Adjustments (Vihara)**
*   **Gentle Yoga:** Specific *asanas* performed under guidance can improve flexibility and strengthen the muscles supporting the joints.
*   **Weight Management:** Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial to reduce the load on your joints, especially the knees and hips.
*   **Stay Warm:** Protect your joints from exposure to cold, as it can significantly increase pain and stiffness.

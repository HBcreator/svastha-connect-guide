**What is Yoga? More Than Just a Physical Exercise**

Originating over 5,000 years ago, Yoga is a holistic science designed to unite the body, mind, and spirit. The word "Yoga" itself means "union" in Sanskrit. While modern interpretations often focus on the physical postures (*asanas*), this is only one of the eight limbs of traditional Yoga.

True Yoga is a comprehensive lifestyle that includes:
*   <b>Asanas (Postures):</b>To build strength, flexibility, and stability in the body.
*   **Pranayama (Breath Control):** Techniques to regulate the breath, which directly influences life force energy (*prana*) and calms the nervous system.
*   **Dhyana (Meditation):** The practice of focusing the mind to achieve a state of deep tranquility.
*   **Ethical Principles (Yamas and Niyamas):** Guidelines for living a conscious and purposeful life.

Yoga is not about performing a perfect handstand; it is about creating balance within, fostering a connection between your physical self and your inner consciousness.

**What is Meditation? The Art of Mindful Stillness**

If Yoga is the journey, Meditation is the destination of inner quiet. Meditation is the practice of training the mind to achieve a state of deep rest, heightened awareness, and peaceful concentration. It is not about "emptying the mind" but rather about observing your thoughts without judgment, allowing them to pass like clouds in the sky.

There are many forms of meditation practiced in India, including:
*   **Vipassana:** A silent meditation focusing on self-observation.
*   **Transcendental Meditation (TM):** Uses a mantra to help the mind settle into a state of deep calm.
*   **Walking Meditation:** A mindful practice of focusing on the physical sensation of walking.

Through consistent practice, meditation helps quiet the mental chatter that often leads to anxiety and stress, fostering a profound sense of peace.

**The Difference Between Yoga and Meditation**

While deeply connected, Yoga and Meditation are distinct. Think of it this way:

*   **Yoga is the broader system; Meditation is a core component of it:** Yoga uses physical postures and breathing techniques to prepare the body and mind *for* meditation. It is difficult to sit still and focus if the body is uncomfortable or the nervous system is agitated. Yoga addresses this by releasing physical tension and calming the mind.
*   **Yoga is active; Meditation is passive:** Yoga involves movement, breathwork, and engagement of the body. Meditation is the art of being still, observing, and allowing the mind to settle.
*   **The Goal:** The physical practice of Yoga aims to create a healthy and stable body, so that one can sit comfortably for long periods of meditation. The ultimate goal of both, however, is the same: to achieve a state of unity and inner harmony.
